    RESEARCH EXPERIENCE

    BSC RESEARCH

    Title: Synthesis of Hydroxyapatite from Snail Shell using Microwave Technique.

    Supervisor: Dr. Yongdan Hou Gbologah
    • To produce Hydroxyapatite from snail shell using the microwave technique and check the effect of varying microwave time through samples characterization.
    • Methodology included burning snail shell through boxed-type electric furnace, grinding and sieving into fine powder through a 300µm sieve.
    • Characterization was performed to check the purity of powder and the availability of Calcium Phosphate which typically is the Hydroxyapatite.
    • The experiment was performed with microwave times of 10, 15, and 20 and was left in an enclosed test tube for particles to settle before it was distilled.
    • The sample was then dried through a laboratory furnace and later grided and sieved once again.
    • Subsequently, small volumes of each were taken to the lab for characterization.
    • The lab resulted suggested that different microwave time has significant effect on the shape of Hydroxyapatite formed with some having an inclusion of OHAP due to higher heating.
    • Microwave synthesis of Hydroxyapatite proved an effective mechanism of producing Hydroxyapatite with consideration given to microwave heating times in order to avoid inclusions.
